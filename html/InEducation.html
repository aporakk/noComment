Classical Literature in Education

Classics are often taught in schools because of their references to the aforementioned events in world history which undoubtedly shaped the world's current social and political systems.

In today's technologically-driven, fast-paced society, it is often difficult to determine the impact that classical literature has on modern day students. Perhaps teens who text message unceasingly as a form of communication could never fathom waiting patiently for a letter, but the bottom line is, most students who dedicate themselves to analyzing these works of literature are likely to extract morals, values, themes, and motifs that are very much still "current" today.

But still, most of the students today can not really appreciate the value of Classical novels, because they don't know the benefits, that they can provide. And still, a lot of teachers involved in the specific subject, can not find the most effective way to provide their knowledge.
